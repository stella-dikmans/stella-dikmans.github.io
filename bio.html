stella dikmans

bio

I am Stella (she/her) – a transdisciplinary sociologist and artistic researcher from Berlin. I am interested in the intersection of technology, creative expression, and its interconnections with (social) spaces. I was born and raised in Berlin to a German mother and a Dutch father as able bodied, white cis person. I started my education at a montessori school in Berlin and then at a secondary reform pedagogical school, ever since then, I have been on a journey of tapping into different interests and passions. I have participated in several development cooperation programmes dealing with human rights, especially education and the (re)construction of social infrastructures.

In Amsterdam, I completed a Bachelor of Science in "Sociology" at the Universiteit van Amsterdam while working in a night café. I was drawn to the discussions and presentations of the social configurations that are constantly in making and transformation, especially in courses on intersectionality and philosophy of ethics. However, I believe I learned as much from the facilities at my university as I did from my work environment, which allowed me to experience (the Dutc) society and its complex layers intimately. I found the rigid and conventional structure of academia to constrain my wish to work in productive collaboration, so I enrolled in the fine arts school Gerrit Rietveld Akademie to pursue a Bachelor of Arts in "Architectural Design". Here, I enjoyed the practical approach towards tackling (social)space related issues and developed an interest in working digitally with various programs and techniques while also getting involved in makerspaces. Theoretically, I became more engaged in writing about decentralizing the human from the design process and recognizing the interconnected whole of the worlds we find ourselves in. I also began questioning status-quo narratives and engaged in decolonial writings and practices. During this time, I was part of various faculty-bodies that dealt with representing the needs of the students and minority groups, where I worked on resolving conflicting interests and mediating room for open dialogue and expressive freedom.

Continuing my path at the Institute for Advanced Architecture of Catalonia in Barcelona, I took part in a one year „Master in Design for Emergent Futures”, that focusses on speculating and prototyping alternative presents. Here, I am introduced to the open-source world and the whole idea of freedom and collaboration the internet was based on once. Through my work, I aim to investigate transformational processes and potential future scenarios that address the intersection of human expression and creativity with artificial intelligence and emerging technologies that are becoming increasingly intertwined. I continue to work with narration and speculative fiction while trying to facilitate space for voices that are often being overheard in the societies I live in.

My practice aims to investigate transformation processes and facilitate spaces for collective (re)contextualisations of pasts, presents, and futures. I am convinced that the thorough study of how human and non-human structures learn and unlearn will feed into our understanding of how they are embedded in a global ever-changing environment – and how possible reactions to these changes could look like. I believe that exchange and discussion are key to opening up new possibilities for seeing and thinking, and promoting heterogeneous coexistences. By studying the uniqueness of these contexts, we start to question and (re)evaluate future options of cohabitation of humans on Earth. Therefore, it seems important to me to examine with critical curiosity various beliefs and certainties that depend on their immediate environment.

statement

I think being part of the worlds I move in comes with a responsibility of finding tools and methods to help navigate the complexities that our neoliberal capitalist system imposes on its people. Together we need to develop a sense of belonging and caring - practices that facilitate spaces for sharing and imagining knowledges that do not yet exist. These spaces must draw on decolonial and ecofeminist thought to combat white heteropatriarchy and oppression.
