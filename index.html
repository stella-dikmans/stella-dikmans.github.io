stella dikmans

Hi, my name is Stella (she/her). I am a 25-year-old German-Dutch individual who grew up in Berlin. With a background in sociology and architectural design, I am passionate about understanding how social structures are embedded in an ever-changing global environment and how we can collectively react to ongoing environmental changes and climate catastrophes. Through my work, I aim to explore transformation processes and investigate possible future scenarios to imagine collective solutions to global challenges. I believe that exchange and discussion are key to opening up new possibilities for seeing and thinking, and promoting heterogeneous coexistences. I am committed to creating social spaces and pushing the boundaries of what's possible.

The Traveller

Working collaboratively, my friend and I joined our research particularly in relation to the changing and complex systems we inhabit, the power dynamics with our fellow critters, and the potential of imagining fictional scenarios. We believe that transition is a vital aspect of this process and have identified three stages: unfamiliarize, grief, and rebirth. By letting go of norms and values, experiencing distress and suffering, and ultimately being liberated by the past, new entities can emerge and grow. Along these line we experiement with facilitating imagination-workshops and engage on multiply dimensions with speculative fiction.

To extend our discussions beyond our social circles, we created an online platform and a traveling sculpture with a QR code leading to the virtual space. Our aim is to inspire a dialogue and engage with a space of thoughts and questions that goes beyond our usual limitations. We hope to diversify the narratives of the future through collective intelligence.

Ghost machine

The project involved repurposing materials from two old computer. The laptop screens, speakers, and LEDs were integrating with an Arduino to create a machine that can detect the presence of people and respond with sound and light expressions. We experiment with various approaches, including using the LED as a sensor and uploading sounds that varied based on the strength of the light sensed.

The result is an installation that aims to blur the lines between the seen and unseen, provides a unique way to engage with the hidden energies in our surroundings, and is a starting point for a fictional imagination of a ghost story.

This ghost machine senses the activity that are not visual to the human eye. A polarized glove allows its wearer to be the channel of energies roaming around freely in air and non-air. With the aid of lightwaves, outer-human existences are communicating and are stating their presence in the betweens.

Watersituationships: An autoethnographic exploration

This is an investigation of the availability and quality of water in Barcelona through experimenting with the first-person perspective method of autoethnography. By living on publicly accessible water for 24 hours without causing the flow of water myself, I tried to become aware of who I share this water with. I try to live as a non-human inhabitant of the city to investigate who has access to fresh water.

My observations show how difficult it is to deviate from an anthropocentric perspective in an environment that is primarily meant for humans. Nevertheless, it raises questions about my (human) average daily water consumption (112 l), the treatment of water for human consumption and the right to define what is “healthy” water and who is allowed to have access to it. For example, what comes out of water treatment plants after being filtered and treated could be considered dead or even poisoned from a nature perspective. I documented my 24-hour challenge in a one-minute video.

50/50 refugee

This piece explores the concept of borders and their impact on society.

In 2021, on 10 May, a military strike kills seven civilians. In the aftermath, another 119 people are killed over a dispute over land, and the right to walk on the streets. Shocked by the unpredictability and spontaneity of these events, I am questioning the made-up lines governments draw on ground and that cause fear and death in so many spaces. This cube floating in the air, blowing with the wind wherever it blows, imagines a safe space where there is no room for fear of soil-related consequences.

I explore the relationship between solids and air, emphasizing that there can be no solids without spaces to fill and no visibility of air and space without solids to give them scale. The piece invites the viewer to consider the role of air as a refuge, as the lighter and potentially more neutral element in contrast to the heavy and divisive nature of frontiers drawn on the ground.
