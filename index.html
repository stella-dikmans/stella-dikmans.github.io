welcome to my performative habitat

stella dikmans

Hi, my name is Stella (she/her). I am a 25-year-old German-Dutch individual who grew up in Berlin. With a background in sociology and architectural design, I am passionate about understanding how social structures are embedded in an ever-changing global environment and how we can collectively react to ongoing environmental changes and climate catastrophes. Through my work, I aim to explore transformation processes and investigate possible future scenarios to imagine collective solutions to global challenges. I believe that exchange and discussion are key to opening up new possibilities for seeing and thinking, and promoting heterogeneous coexistences. I am committed to creating social spaces and pushing the boundaries of what's possible.

50/50 refuge

This piece explores the concept of borders and their impact on society. In 2021, on 10 May, a military strike kills seven civilians. In the aftermath, another 119 people are killed over a dispute over land, and the right to walk on the streets. Shocked by the unpredictability and spontaneity of these events, I am questioning the made-up lines governments draw on ground and that cause fear and death in so many spaces. This cube floating in the air, blowing with the wind wherever it blows, imagines a safe space where there is no room for fear of soil-related consequences. In this work I explore the relationship between solids and air, emphasizing that there can be no solids without spaces to fill and no visibility of air and space without solids to give them scale. The piece invites the viewer to consider the role of air as a refuge, as the lighter and potentially more neutral element in contrast to the heavy and divisive nature of frontiers drawn on the ground.
